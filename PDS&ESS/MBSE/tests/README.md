# PDS & ESS IVV-aligned verification plan and incremental gate index

Planning and definition-index artifact for the PDS & ESS Capella/D2 views. It continues to inventory the `PDS&ESS/MBSE/v1.0` final flight acceptance model, and now also indexes incremental development-version gates such as `v0.1` and `v0.2`. The model remains the source of truth; this file tracks IVV coverage and points to verification-specific model definitions created under this `tests/` folder. Baseline source views in their version folders are not modified by these test definitions.

Project-wide IVV conventions, evidence paths, statistical policy, fault semantics, and report-by-reference rules are defined in [`../../../../PM&SE/IVV.md`](../../../../PM&SE/IVV.md). Evidence for any activity below should be stored under `results/<activity-id>/` in this `tests/` folder unless a campaign-level folder is later agreed.

## 1. SSIV / development-version assumptions

- **Primary SSIV for the original v1.0 definition pass:** PDS & ESS subsystem integration version **v1.0 final flight acceptance**.
- **Incremental development-version gates indexed:** `PDS-V01-GATE-001` covers the PDS & ESS **v0.1** power-path advancement gate toward v0.2; `PDS-VV-V02-FC-001` covers the PDS & ESS **v0.2** INA219 measurement/reporting advancement gate toward v0.3; `PDS-V03-GATE-001` covers the PDS & ESS **v0.3** protection/endurance advancement gate toward v1.0.
- **Version semantics:** earlier PDS & ESS versions (`v0.1`, `v0.2`, `v0.3`) are protoflight/development baselines. Their verification definitions and evidence shall not be used as substitutes for v1.0 final flight acceptance evidence.
- **Safety assumption:** local Li-ion tests are normal charge/discharge or battery-emulator/current-limited tests only. Destructive abuse evidence must come from certified battery/protection documentation or an accredited lab.
- **Fault semantics from project IVV:** INA219 startup failure is a critical peripheral startup fault; runtime getter/sensor faults shall return bounded error/result codes and shall not block the scheduler.

## 4. Planning inventory from the model

### 4.1 Physical context and components

Physical context: `[System] CanSat`. External actor in the physical view: `[EA] Charger`.

### 4.2 Physical links

USB-C cable; battery cable; ESS backplane fixture; PDS backplane fixture; OBCC backplane fixture; protection footprint; INA219 footprint; regulator footprint; battery-connector footprint; PDS backplane-connector footprint; XIAO footprint; OBCC backplane-connector footprint; PDS PCB screws; OBCC PCB screws; battery ± traces; protection-circuit output trace; INA output trace; 5V/3V3 regulator/backplane traces; OBCC 3V3/PWM traces; PDS parallel cable; OBCC parallel cable.

### 4.3 Component exchanges

- `[CE] Pointers`: OBCC to ESS Processing.
- `[CE] Returns`: ESS Processing to OBCC.
- **Modeling gap:** the battery-supervision chain and timing constraints imply INA219 I2C communication, but the logical view does not show a `[CE] I2C` between Battery monitoring/INA219 and ESS Processing/XIAO.

### 4.4 Logical components and allocated functions

- `[EA] Charger`: `Provide power`.
- `[EA] User`: `Switch power on`, `Switch power off`.
- `[LC] Battery`: `Store energy`, `Provide power`.
- `[LC] Battery Charger`: `Regulate voltage`.
- `[LC] Power Protection`: `Detect overcurrent`, `Detect overvoltage`, `Restore power`, `Cut off power`.
- `[LC] Battery monitoring`: `Measure voltage`, `Measure current`, `Respond to I2C requests`.
- `[LC] Regulator`: `Regulate voltage`.
- `[LC] ESS Processing`: `Get INA219 init state`, `Read voltage`, `Read current`, `Process voltage`, `Process current`.
- `[LC] OBCC`: `Collect peripheral init states`, `Collect measurements`.

### 4.5 Functional chains / scenarios

- `PDS v1.0 — Power and protection chain`: charge, store, provide, regulate, switch on/off, restore, detect overcurrent/overvoltage, and cut off power.
- `PDS v1.0 — Battery supervision chain`: INA219 init/read, voltage/current measurement, I2C response, processing, and OBCC collection.
- Implied safety scenarios requiring later definition: Li-ion documentation review, overload/short/overvoltage protection demonstrations, hot/vibration/drop environmental checks.

### 4.6 Constraints

Charge at `4.2 V, 0.5 A`; enough energy for `6 hours` continuous operation in On mode; protection probability `< 1%`; supply both `5±0.1 V` and `3.3±0.1 V` at `1 A`; variable-getter template for read/process pairs; process/calculate functions `< 5 ms`; I2C read timeout `≤ 5 ms`; no blocking operations besides I2C or UART comms; all PCBs machinable in a Carvera Air CNC.

### 4.7 Traceability targets currently available

The PDS & ESS views do not contain explicit mission, capability, use-case, or feared-event nodes. Candidate trace targets used below are provisional: `PDS-UC-ChargeESS`, `PDS-UC-ProvideRegulatedPower`, `PDS-UC-SuperviseBattery`, `PDS-FE-LiIonUnsafeCondition`, `PDS-FE-OvercurrentOvervoltage`, `PDS-FE-RailBrownout`, `PDS-FE-SensorBusFault`, and the modeled constraints listed above.

## 6. Coverage and gaps

- **Incremental v0.1 gate coverage:** `PDS-V01-GATE-001` provides the IVV-indexed v0.1-to-v0.2 advancement definition covering all v0.1 PCs/PLs, verification-only component exchanges, source function allocations, power-chain smoke sequence, safe power-off, and the screw-terminal/cable constraint. Execution evidence is pending; `PDS-V01-GATE-001` remains characterization-only screening for rail/current performance because v0.1 has no formal rail tolerance/current constraint in the baseline model.
- **Incremental v0.2 gate coverage:** `PDS-VV-V02-FC-001` provides the v0.2-to-v0.3 measurement/reporting advancement definition covering relevant v0.2 PCs/PLs, verification component exchanges, Setup/Loop/Battery monitoring allocations, the INA219 measurement/reporting chain, the I2C timeout/no-blocking fault-hardening chain, and all v0.2 timing/API/cadence/unused-field constraints. Execution evidence is pending; this is an incremental development gate and not a substitute for v1.0 final flight acceptance evidence.
- **Incremental v0.3 gate coverage:** `PDS-V03-GATE-001` provides the v0.3 protection/endurance advancement definition for power-path safety, demanded protection (overcurrent and overvoltage), battery-supervision regression, and 6-hour On-mode endurance constraints using constrained conservative statistics. Coverage includes model PCs/PLs, verification-only fixtures and links, allocation, fault chains, and endurance loop constraints. Execution evidence is pending; this is also an incremental development gate and not a substitute for v1.0 final flight acceptance evidence.
- **Physical coverage:** all PDS & ESS v1.0 physical components and physical links are covered by PDS-VV-PC-001, PDS-VV-PL-001, and the Li-ion safety review PDS-VV-SAFE-001. A modeled inspection definition now exists at `tests/PDS-VV-PC-001/`.
- **Exchange coverage:** all modeled component exchanges are covered by PDS-VV-CE-001 and PDS-VV-CE-002; their supporting analysis is now modeled in `tests/PDS-VV-FC-002/`. PDS-VV-CE-003 identifies the missing but implied INA219 I2C CE and remains blocked for source-model completeness until resolved or justified.
- **Allocation coverage:** all modeled logical components, external-actor functions, and allocated functions are covered by PDS-VV-ALLOC-001 through PDS-VV-ALLOC-003. Battery-monitoring/ESS Processing/OBCC allocation analysis support is now modeled in `tests/PDS-VV-FC-002/`.
- **Functional-chain coverage:** both modeled chains are covered by PDS-VV-FC-001 and PDS-VV-FC-002. Modeled definitions now exist for the full power/protection chain at `tests/PDS-VV-FC-001/` and for the battery-supervision chain at `tests/PDS-VV-FC-002/`.
- **Constraint coverage:** every explicit PDS & ESS constraint is assigned to at least one constraint-derived activity. Modeled definitions now exist for the full power/protection chain constraints through `tests/PDS-VV-FC-001/`, for the normal charge constraint at `tests/PDS-VV-CON-001/`, for the 6-hour On-mode endurance constraint at `tests/PDS-VV-CON-002/`, for the protection probability constraint at `tests/PDS-VV-CON-003/`, for the regulated-rail constraint at `tests/PDS-VV-CON-004/`, and for the variable-getter, process-time, I2C-timeout, and no-blocking constraints through `tests/PDS-VV-FC-002/`.
- **Modeling gaps:** explicit mission/capability/use-case/feared-event nodes are absent; the INA219 I2C component exchange is still missing from the source logical view; the source meaning of `Probability < 1%` remains ambiguous but `PDS-VV-CON-003` and `PDS-V03-GATE-001` define and trace a conservative missed-demand interpretation for verification; execution of `PDS-VV-CON-002` requires a campaign-confirmed On-mode profile/current budget; master switch/accessible safety switch hardware is not explicit in the physical view.
- **Remaining definition gaps:** power allocation review, external actor allocation review, and PCB manufacturability/coupon evidence definitions still need detailed modeled procedures or campaign parameters. For `PDS-VV-CON-003`, execution still needs campaign setpoints, independence rationale, and actual demand-trial evidence. For `PDS-VV-CON-001`, execution still needs charger model/certification, cell safety-document records, campaign tolerance/uncertainty, and actual charge-profile evidence.
